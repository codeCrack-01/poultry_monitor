# Poultry Farm Monitoring System - UX Design Brief

## Project Overview

We are building a mobile-first poultry farm monitoring system for farm owners and managers.

The goal is not to create a technical sensor dashboard. The goal is to allow a farm owner to understand the health of their poultry farm in under 5 seconds and quickly identify where a problem exists.

Most users will access the system from Android phones while working on-site. Desktop support is secondary.

The system monitors environmental conditions inside poultry sheds and provides real-time operational visibility.

---

## Primary Users

### Farm Owner

Owns one or more farms.

Needs to know:

* Is any farm in trouble?
* Which area has a problem?
* How severe is the problem?
* Do I need to take action immediately?

The owner does NOT care about raw sensor values unless troubleshooting.

---

### Farm Manager / Operator

Responsible for investigating issues.

Needs additional visibility into:

* Which zone has problems
* Which fan may be malfunctioning
* Which environmental parameter triggered the alert

---

## Business Context

Current solutions are expensive, hardware-locked, and difficult to monitor.

Many farms rely on:

* Manual inspections
* CCTV monitoring
* Human observation

The proposed system should provide a simpler and cheaper alternative.

---

# Core Metrics

The system currently monitors:

1. Temperature
2. Humidity
3. Ammonia Concentration
4. Generator Status
5. Fan Status

No additional metrics should be assumed.

---

# Mental Model

The system should be designed around physical locations rather than sensors.

Users think:

"Zone D is overheating."

NOT:

"Sensor #17 is reporting 34°C."

The UI should therefore prioritize spatial understanding.

---

# Physical Structure

Owner
└── Farm(s)
└── Shed
└── Zones
├── Fans
└── Sensors

A farm owner may own multiple farms.

Each farm currently contains a single poultry shed.

Each shed contains multiple zones.

Each zone contains:

* One or more fans
* Multiple environmental sensors

---

# Zone Concept

A Zone represents a physical region of the shed.

A zone is not necessarily a wall-separated area.

A zone is an operational area influenced by one or more fans and measured by nearby sensors.

The system should aggregate environmental conditions at the zone level.

Examples:

Zone A

* Fan Group 1
* Temperature Sensors
* Humidity Sensors
* Ammonia Sensors

Zone B

* Fan Group 2
* Temperature Sensors
* Humidity Sensors
* Ammonia Sensors

etc.

---

# Design Philosophy

The UI should answer operational questions before showing technical details.

The user should not need to interpret raw sensor readings.

The interface should prioritize:

1. Health Status
2. Location of Problems
3. Severity
4. Actionability

Raw sensor data should be hidden behind drill-down screens.

---

# UX Questions The Design Must Answer

## Question 1

Is any farm unhealthy?

The user should be able to answer this immediately after opening the app.

---

## Question 2

Which farm requires attention?

The problematic farm should be obvious.

---

## Question 3

Which area of the shed is affected?

Users should quickly identify the problematic zone.

---

## Question 4

What is causing the problem?

Possible examples:

* High temperature
* High ammonia
* Fan failure
* Generator failure

---

## Question 5

How severe is the problem?

Visual severity indicators should be clear.

Examples:

Green = Normal

Yellow = Warning

Red = Critical

---

# Information Hierarchy

## Level 1

Farm Overview

Purpose:
Show health status of all farms.

---

## Level 2

Farm Health Dashboard

Purpose:
Show overall environmental condition of the selected farm.

Display:

* Temperature
* Humidity
* Ammonia
* Generator Status
* Fan Status

---

## Level 3

Shed Zone View

Purpose:
Display the shed as a collection of zones.

Users should immediately identify problematic areas.

This is expected to be one of the most important screens.

---

## Level 4

Zone Detail View

Purpose:
Show detailed information about a specific zone.

Display:

* Temperature
* Humidity
* Ammonia
* Fan Status
* Recent alerts

Only here should device-level details begin to appear.

---

## Level 5

Alerts

Purpose:
Provide a chronological list of incidents and warnings.

Examples:

* High Ammonia
* High Temperature
* Fan Offline
* Generator Offline

---

# What Should NOT Be Prioritized

Avoid designing around:

* Sensor IDs
* Technical diagnostics
* Electrical values
* ADC readings
* Configuration screens
* Complex analytics

This is an operational monitoring product, not an engineering console.

---

# Desired Outcome

The final design should feel closer to:

* Industrial monitoring systems
* Operational control dashboards
* SCADA-inspired monitoring interfaces

and less like:

* Business analytics dashboards
* CRM systems
* Generic SaaS admin panels

The user should be able to determine the operational health of a farm in seconds with minimal training.
